The €2M Recovery Mission

How a 90-day turnaround saved a top-tier contract and redefined strategic quality leadership.

The Challenge

Our Class 2 cleanroom line was producing PCBA assemblies with a failure rate of 9.56%. This posed a €2M business risk through increased scrap and rework, delayed deliveries, and damaged client confidence.

A critical audit was approaching and the client issued a conditional warning: fix the issue within 90 days or risk losing future orders.

Strategic Approach

Phase 1

Root Cause Mapping

MES data + Gemba walks + Ishikawa exposed ambiguous work instructions as a top issue, affecting shift consistency.

Phase 2

CAPA Governance

Daily tier meetings aligned with visual dashboard. Ownership and KPIs were defined for every CAPA action.

Phase 3

Precision Execution

Used existing resources, trained all shifts under IPC Class 2 and redesigned inspection flows — all without extra budget.

Sustaining the Gains

Process Integration

CAPAs embedded into Daily Management Boards with clear ownership and measurable KPIs.

Knowledge Transfer

Reinforced multilingual training programs across all production shifts.

Standardization

Deployed standardized checklists and implemented recurring visual audits.

Performance Tracking

Implemented live dashboards for tracking FPY and potential failure modes.

Career Impact

This project redefined my role from executor to strategic quality leader — orchestrating cross-functional initiatives, influencing with data, and delivering transformation under pressure.

The methodologies developed during this critical recovery mission have since become standard practice across multiple production lines, contributing to sustained quality improvements company-wide.
